# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Residents and community members of Davao City, Mindanao, Philippines. They want to understand flood risk at a specific location without interpreting raw ML metrics. Their context is personal: checking their home, a property they plan to buy, or a familiar area. The interface must feel approachable and trustworthy, not academic or clinical.

## Product Purpose

Davao FloodSense provides flood susceptibility mapping using ensemble machine learning (Random Forest + XGBoost). It turns complex terrain and environmental data into a clear, actionable risk assessment. The goal is to make flood risk information accessible to people who are not data scientists: residents, community planners, and local stakeholders.

## Brand Personality

Community-focused, warm, clear. Think public service information that respects the user's intelligence. Trustworthy without being cold. Authoritative without being academic. The feel of a well-designed public service tool, not a commercial SaaS dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with gray sidebars and metric card grids. This is not a business analytics tool.
- Dense academic or government-style pages with walls of text and small labels.
- Flashy "tech startup" design with gradient text, glassmorphism, or heavy animations. The subject deserves seriousness.

## Design Principles

1. **The map is the interface.** Location selection drives everything. The sidebar supports the map, not the other way around.
2. **Interpret, don't expose.** Users should be told what the data means, not handed numbers and expected to figure it out.
3. **Confidence without coldness.** The tone is authoritative but never clinical. Flood risk is a serious subject; the UI should treat it that way without feeling sterile.
4. **Color is semantic.** Color carries meaning (risk levels, guidance, states) and must work for color blind users. Never use red/green as the only distinction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Color blind safe: risk levels are distinguished by pattern, position, and text label in addition to color. Support reduced motion.
